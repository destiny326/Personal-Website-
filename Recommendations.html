Documentary Recommendations

Featured Picks

Here are the documentaries I think everyone should watch — not just because they're good, but because they actually leave something behind. These aren't ranked, just solid picks that stuck with me.

  1. The Act of Killing (2012)

    2. Disturbing and brilliant. Forces you to look at the banality of evil in a way few films dare to.

  2. Won't You Be My Neighbor? (2018)

    3. A reminder that kindness still matters — and that we've lost a bit of it.

  3. 13th (2016)

    4. A powerful, angry, necessary look at how the prison system replaced slavery in America.

  4. Free Solo (2018)

    5. Insane and beautiful. A look into obsession, fear, and what drives someone to climb a mountain without ropes.

  5. My Octopus Teacher (2020)

    6. Gentle and emotional — not for everyone, but a quiet story about connection and burnout.
