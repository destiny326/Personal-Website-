Horror Recommendations

Featured Picks

Here are some horror films and series that left their mark — not because of jump scares, but because they linger long after the credits roll. These aren't ranked, just the ones that stayed in my head a little too long.

  1. Eli (2019)

    2. A boy trapped in a sterile house under “treatment” for his illness — until he realizes the real sickness isn't in him. Twisted, claustrophobic, and cruelly clever.

  2. The Haunting of Hill House (2018)

    3. Grief, trauma, and ghosts — all tangled into one heartbreaking story. Less about monsters and more about what haunts families forever.

  3. The Woman in the Yard (2024)

    4. A slow-burn psychological thriller that turns domestic comfort into creeping paranoia. It's the kind of story that feels too real to shake off.

  4. The Other Side of the Door (2016)

    5. Grief opens doors that should stay closed. A haunting take on loss and the price of not letting go — emotionally charged and genuinely eerie.

  5. The Haunting of Bly Manor (2020)

    6. A gothic love story disguised as a ghost story. Elegant, tragic, and full of emotional weight — a haunting that hurts in all the right ways.
