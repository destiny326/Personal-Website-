Thoughts

  1. Talk to Me

    2. Bleak, shocking, and surprisingly emotional. It's the rare possession movie that actually has something to say — about grief, guilt, and losing control.

  2. Hereditary

    3. Ari Aster's slow, suffocating masterpiece. The real horror isn't the demon — it's watching a family fall apart piece by piece.

  3. The Witch

    4. A cold, quiet descent into madness. A film that feels like an unearthed 17th-century nightmare — haunting and authentic.

  4. Barbarian

    5. Starts one way, flips completely, and still lands hard. Twisted and clever — a must-watch if you like your horror unpredictable.

  5. It Follows

    6. One of the most original horror concepts in years. Relentless, moody, and proof that simplicity can be terrifying.
